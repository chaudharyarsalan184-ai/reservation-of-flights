The state capital of Texas, Austin, is a relatively busy place. It was recently voted the top site to live in America. Austin is lauded for its warm weather, affordable housing, and bustling cultural scene. From densely packed urban areas to family-friendly suburbs, housing options are ideal here in Austin. It is a city bursting with an entrepreneurial spirit, a passion for unearthing discoveries, and a commitment to personal freedom. Austin lives up to its reputation of being the world's live music capital by housing 200 venues with more than dozens of shows every day and night. Austin is the perfect city for the active lifestyle, from calm and clear spring-fed waterbeds to lush, shady Greenbelt trails.


Top things to do in Austin:

Blanton Museum of Art:Get ready for a world-class art experience in the heart of Austin. This museum holds art exhibitions that express emotions through arts and speak to the audience differently. Some of its most popular exhibitions are Leo Steinberg's Library of prints, Diedrick Brakens' darling divined, and many more. If you have a keen eye for art, the Blanton Museum of Art is the place for you.


Zilker Park: Considered Austin's most beloved park, Zilker Park is a recreational area in South Austin at Barton Creek and the Colorado River. It comprises about 350 acres of publicly owned land, named after its benefactor, Andrew Jackson Zilker, who donated the land to the city in 1917. The park features three picnic sites and a shelter, Zilker playscape, Disc Golf Course, 5 Volleyball courts, Zilker Clubhouse, and many more delightful experiences.


Lady Bird Lake:Lady Bird Lake is a river-like reservoir on the Colorado River in Austin. The lake was created in 1960 as a cooling pond for a new city power plant. The lake, which has a surface area of 416 acres, is now used primarily for recreation and flood control. It is located on a fun hike-and-bike trail which makes the view even more scenic.


Bullock Texas State History Museum:The Bullock Texas State History Museum is a history museum in Austin. The museum is located a few blocks north of the Texas State Capitol at 1800 North Congress Avenue in Austin, is dedicated to interpreting the continually unfolding "Story of Texas" to the broadest possible audience through meaningful educational experiences.
