Apart from the food and jazz music Chicago is famous for its architecture like Sears Tower and museums and its loyal sports fans. It is one of America's largest metropolises, offering an incredible medley of modern wonders and cultural offerings.


What to do in Chicago:

Take a walk through Millennium Park, often called the city’s “front lawn,” where some of Chicago’s most iconic landmarks and landscapes await. Wander through the vibrant Lurie Garden, surrounded by a striking 15-foot hedge, explore the rotating art installations, and enjoy the seasonal exhibitions that bring the park to life year-round.


Be captivated by the Adler Planetarium, a historic science museum established in 1930 at the northeastern edge of Northerly Island. Its classic planetarium dome and Art Deco design, adorned with bronze zodiac motifs, give it a timeless appeal. Step inside the dome to experience the Sky Theater, where dazzling digital projections take you on an unforgettable journey through the universe.
